00Why this exists — the cold start
The first sixty seconds decide whether a person stays. This is what makes them not
feel empty.
A user connects Gmail through OAuth. Within the first minute — before they search for a
single email — a large share of them will type some version of one question: "who am I?" ·
"what do you think of me?" · "tell me something about myself." That moment is the whole
product. It is the screenshot moment, the moment that decides whether they ever come
back.
And at that exact moment, the graph is nearly empty. No time has passed. Nothing has
accumulated. If Chronic can only speak from what this user's data has taught it so far, the first
answer is thin — and a thin answer to the most important question, on the first try, kills the user
before the product ever gets the chance to be good.
ChatGPT isn't smart because of its users. It's smart because it read
the books first. Chronic, today, has no books.
This library is the books. It is a set of recognizable life-shapes — the founder's loop, the
homecoming arc, the gap between what a person says and what they do — that a brand-new user
is matched against from day one. The user shows up with two weeks of mail, and instead of
starting from zero, Chronic asks: "which of the shapes I already know does this resemble?" — and
it has a head start. The library is what lets a nearly-empty graph still say something that makes a
person stop and think "how does it know that already?"
TWO LIBRARIES, NOT ONE — AND ONLY ONE OF THEM IS NOW
The mined library — learning new life-shapes from thousands of users — needs the users,
and is later. The seeded library — the shapes EYES carries into the first conversation, hand-
authored before a single user shows up — is needed now, because it is the only thing that
makes minute-one land. This document is the seeded library. It is the fifty years of life pre-
loaded into the technological infant.

01The seven disciplines
A pattern library is one bad step from horoscopes. These are the rules that keep it
true.
The danger is real and specific. A library of life-shapes that "match" people becomes
astrology the moment the shapes are vague enough to fit anyone, or the moment a
psychological label gets stamped on a person from two weeks of email. EYES's entire
worth is being true and evidenced about a specific person. Every pattern in this document
obeys all seven of these, or it does not go in the library.
A pattern is a prior, not a label. It is a starting hypothesis to be confirmed or killed against the
person's real data — never a verdict stamped on them. The library makes minute-one non-
empty. The person's own data is what makes it true.
1
Every pattern is grounded in detectable signals. If a shape cannot be tied to concrete
features the Entity, Relationship, and Temporal engines actually produce — mention
frequencies, relation-type ratios, delayed_on counts, drift over time, co-occurrence — it
does not go in the library. No vibes. This is the line between EYES and a fortune teller.
2
Anchoring still applies. When Chronic surfaces a pattern match, it cites the evidence — the
same receipt rule as every other claim. "I think you might run on external deadlines — here
are the three commitments that show it." Never a free-floating insight.
3
Confidence matches the data. A match on two weeks of one source is a hypothesis, and
must be spoken as one. The uncertainty lives in the phrasing — "early read," "tentative," "tell
me if I'm wrong," "I'll get sharper." Overclaiming a pattern on thin data is the single thing that
breaks trust on the first answer.
4
Patterns must be falsifiable. Every pattern names what would disconfirm it. A shape that
matches everyone is a Barnum statement — worthless and dishonest. If it cannot be wrong,
it is not in the library.
5
The wellbeing discipline. Some shapes touch distress — isolation, narrowing, a fading
relationship, burnout. These are surfaced with the most care: as gentle observations oriented
toward the person's own reflection, never as alarming diagnoses, never assuming the cause,
and — when the signals are strong and concerning — leaning toward encouraging real
human connection rather than cold pattern-reporting. EYES calls a person out on their
avoidance. It does not bludgeon them about their pain. That line is real, and the library holds
it.
6
Seeded now, mined later. This v0 is hand-authored from knowledge and behavioral
observation. Over time, the privacy-preserved aggregate — the shape of patterns across
many lives, never the contents of any one — will surface new shapes to add. The seeded
library solves the cold start today; the mined library deepens it later.

02How each pattern is built
The anatomy of a card — so the structure is reusable as you add your own.
Every pattern in the next section is one card with the same parts. The structure is the
discipline — it forces each shape to be grounded, voiced honestly, and falsifiable. When
you add your own patterns, fill these same fields, or the new pattern doesn't earn its
place.
NAME + CODE The shape's plain name and an internal codename. Internal only — the user
never sees "you are exhibiting Pattern A2."
SENSITIVITY Standard, Sensitive, or Positive. Sensitive shapes carry a handling note and
are surfaced with care, never as alarm.
THE SHAPE What this life-pattern actually is, in one or two plain sentences.
SIGNALS IT CAN DETECT The concrete graph features that suggest the shape — frequencies, ratios,
edge types, drift. This is the grounding. No signal, no pattern.
THE COLD-START READ What Chronic might actually say on day one when it matches — in the EYES
voice, perceptive and honest, hedged for thin data, never flattering, never a
verdict.
CONFIRMS / DISCONFIRMS What later data would strengthen the hypothesis, and what would kill it. This
is the falsifiability — and the hook into the prediction-and-outcome learning
loop.
ON THE VOICE IN THE COLD-START READS
The reads below are written in EYES's register: direct, specific, perceptive, and humane —
the voice that calls a person out without coddling them, and without ever pretending to
certainty it doesn't have. They are illustrations of the register, not scripts to ship word-for-
word. The names in brackets are placeholders the engine fills from the person's real entities.

03The patterns — v0
Fifteen life-shapes across six categories. The starter set. Yours to refine.
A · HOW A PERSON BUILDS the shape of how they make things
A1The Builder's LoopTHE_LOOP STANDARD
A person who cycles idea →  deep research →  architecture →  build →  near-launch →  new,
bigger idea, rarely closing the loop on the last thing before the next one captures them.
SIGNALS IT CAN DETECT
Multiple project entities, each reaching a "build" stage; new project entities appearing before prior ones
reach a shipped/closed event; high density of research and learning topic mentions relative to completed-
milestone events; committed_to edges on projects with no matching completion.
THE COLD-START READ
"From your mail alone, I can already see at least three things you started with real intensity —
[X], [Y], [Z]. Two of them go quiet right around the point the third one starts. Could be
coincidence. Could be a pattern worth watching. I'll know more as I see more."
CONFIRMS IF
more projects follow the same start-intensify-
abandon-restart shape over months.
DISCONFIRMS IF
projects reach sustained shipped states; the last
thing gets finished before the next begins.
A2Executes for Others, Orbits Own WorkORBIT STANDARD
Delivers reliably on commitments to other people, but commitments to their own work and goals
stall, circle, or never get the same follow-through.
SIGNALS IT CAN DETECT
High completion rate on committed_to edges whose tail is another person/organization; low
completion on committed_to edges whose tail is a self-goal or own project; delayed_on concentrated
on self-directed commitments; emotional_state around own work skewing anxious or avoidant.
THE COLD-START READ
"Early read, just from your email: when someone else is waiting on you, you deliver. When it's
your own thing, it tends to orbit. The commitments with a name attached get done; the ones
that are only yours get circled. Tell me if that lands, or if I'm reading it wrong."
CONFIRMS IF
the others-vs-self completion gap holds as more
commitments resolve.
DISCONFIRMS IF
self-directed goals complete at a similar rate to
commitments made to others.

A3The Perfectionist HoldHOLD STANDARD
Work reaches near-completion and then stalls indefinitely in refinement, never quite crossing
into release.
SIGNALS IT CAN DETECT
project entities with many revision/edit events but no release/publish event; long-lived valid_to-null
commitments to "finishing" a thing; a project's mention frequency staying high without any completion
event closing it.
THE COLD-START READ
"One thing I notice early: [project] has been close to done for a while. Lots of polishing, not
much shipping. I might be missing the moment it went out — but if it hasn't, that gap between
'almost' and 'out' is worth a look."
CONFIRMS IF
multiple projects show the same long refine-
without-release tail.
DISCONFIRMS IF
work ships at a normal cadence; "almost done"
reliably becomes "out."
B · COMMITMENT & FOLLOW-THROUGH the gap between saying and doing
B1Runs on External AccountabilityEXTERNAL STANDARD
Things with an external deadline or someone watching get done; things with neither don't —
regardless of how important the person says they are.
SIGNALS IT CAN DETECT
Completion events cluster around commitments that have an associated deadline event or other-person
tail; self-stated important goals without external markers accumulate delayed_on; the gap between
stated priority and completion correlates with the presence or absence of an external watcher.
THE COLD-START READ
"Here's an early pattern, and it's a common one: the things on your plate that have a deadline
or a person attached get done. The ones that are just important to you — no deadline, nobody
waiting — those are the ones that slip. The work has someone watching. The personal stuff has
neither."
CONFIRMS IF
presence/absence of an external marker keeps
predicting completion.
DISCONFIRMS IF
unwatched personal goals complete at the same
rate as deadline-bound ones.

B2The Quiet NoQUIET_NO STANDARD
Soft agreement to things that then never get acted on — a "yes" that was never really a
commitment, just a way to end the conversation.
SIGNALS IT CAN DETECT
committed_to edges followed by no action and eventual silence — decay, not an explicit
decided_against; recurring delayed_on on a particular category of ask; a gap between the language of
agreement and any downstream event.
THE COLD-START READ
"Tentative, because I need more to be sure: there's a kind of 'yes' in your mail that doesn't seem
to turn into anything — agreements that just quietly go nowhere. Not refusals. Just yeses that
weren't really yeses. Worth noticing which asks those are."
CONFIRMS IF
a consistent class of agreements keeps dissolving
into silence.
DISCONFIRMS IF
stated agreements generally produce follow-up
action.
C · IDENTITY & TRANSITION who a person is becoming, or leaving behind
C1The Homecoming ArcHOMECOMING SENSITIVE
A person who moved away from an origin — a place, a culture, a former life — and whose data
shows a slow gravitational pull back toward it, often beneath their stated direction.
SIGNALS IT CAN DETECT
Rising mention frequency of origin place and origin person entities over time, while the current place's
emotional_state valence flattens or declines; committed_to edges toward the current direction
accumulating delayed_on; co-occurrence of the origin place with family and belonging entities.
THE COLD-START READ
"I don't have enough yet to say much here, and I want to be careful — but one early thread:
[origin] comes up more than I'd expect for someone whose plans seem to point elsewhere. I'm
not concluding anything. I'll just watch whether that holds, and you can tell me what it means."
CONFIRMS IF
origin mentions keep rising as forward-direction
commitments keep slipping.
DISCONFIRMS IF
origin mentions are stable/declining and the
current direction is progressing.
HANDLING
Touches belonging and possibly homesickness. Surface as a gentle observation about the data, never as
"you secretly want to go home." Let the person assign the meaning.

C2The ReinventionREINVENTION STANDARD
A person actively migrating away from a former identity or field — the old vocabulary fading as
a new one rises.
SIGNALS IT CAN DETECT
An old topic/skill cluster declining in mention frequency over time (downward drift) while a new cluster
rises (upward drift); decided_against edges on the old direction; new project and goal entities
concentrated in the new domain.
THE COLD-START READ
"Something's shifting in what you spend words on. [Old domain] is fading; [new domain] is
taking its place — and it doesn't look accidental. If you're in the middle of becoming a different
kind of person professionally, the data already shows it before you'd probably say it out loud."
CONFIRMS IF
the old cluster keeps declining and the new one
keeps rising over time.
DISCONFIRMS IF
both clusters stay active, or the "old" one re-rises
— that's C3, not reinvention.
C3Caught BetweenLIMINAL SENSITIVE
A person genuinely suspended between two places, careers, or identities — neither resolving.
Sustained tension, not migration.
SIGNALS IT CAN DETECT
Two competing entity clusters both active over time, neither declining; conflicts_with edges between
stated intentions; oscillating emotional_state valence rather than a settling trend.
THE COLD-START READ
"Early, and gently: you seem to be holding two things at once — [A] and [B] — and neither is
winning. That in-between can be its own kind of heavy. I'm not sure yet whether it's a phase or
a place you're stuck in. I'll watch, and you can tell me which it is."
CONFIRMS IF
both clusters stay active with no resolution over a
long window.
DISCONFIRMS IF
one cluster clearly wins out — that's reinvention or
homecoming resolving.
HANDLING
Liminal states can carry real anxiety. Acknowledge the weight without dramatizing it; never imply the
person is failing to choose. Observation, not pressure.

D1High-Leverage, Low-ContactHIGH_LEVERAGE POSITIVE
A person who appears in a small number of high-consequence moments — decisions,
opportunities, turning points — but a low overall volume of communication.
SIGNALS IT CAN DETECT
A person entity with few total mentions but high co-occurrence with decision, opportunity, and milestone
event entities; appears disproportionately at temporal inflection points in the graph.
THE COLD-START READ
"Here's one I find interesting already: [Name] barely shows up in your email by volume — but
the few times they do, something important is happening around it. Most people never notice
who their low-contact, high-leverage people are. [Name] looks like one of yours."
CONFIRMS IF
the person keeps surfacing at consequential
moments despite low volume.
DISCONFIRMS IF
their appearances turn out to be routine, not tied
to consequence.
D2The Fading ThreadFADING SENSITIVE
A person whose interaction has steadily declined over time — a relationship cooling, often
without the person consciously noticing.
SIGNALS IT CAN DETECT
Declining mention frequency of a person entity across successive periods; lengthening gaps between
interactions; a valid_to-null relationship edge with no recent supporting events.
THE COLD-START READ
"One quiet thing, and I'll hold it lightly: you and [Name] used to be in contact a lot more than
you are now. The thread's gone thin. I don't know why — people drift, life happens, and
sometimes it's nothing. Just something you might not have clocked."
CONFIRMS IF
the decline continues across later periods.
DISCONFIRMS IF
contact resumes, or the lull was a known,
temporary gap.
HANDLING
A fade can mean grief, estrangement, or loss. Never assume the reason, never imply fault, never push
"you should reach out." Offer the observation; let the person decide what it is.

E1The Stated-Goal / Actual-Behavior GapSAY_DO_GAP SENSITIVE
A clear, repeatedly stated intention with little or no corresponding action — the gap between
what a person says they're doing and what the record actually shows. The accountability core
of EYES.
SIGNALS IT CAN DETECT
A goal or commitment entity with high mention frequency (talked about often) but near-zero associated
action or completion events; delayed_on accumulating; conflicts_with between the stated goal and
where time and attention actually go.
THE COLD-START READ
"Early, tentative, and tell me if it's unfair: [goal] comes up a lot in how you talk — but I'm not yet
seeing much in the record that looks like moving on it. Could be I just can't see the action.
Could be something else. I'll get sharper, and I'd rather be corrected than wrong."
CONFIRMS IF
the talk-without-action gap persists as more data
arrives.
DISCONFIRMS IF
action on the goal shows up that the thin early data
simply hadn't surfaced yet.
HANDLING
This is the insight most likely to sting, and the one most likely to be wrong on thin data. Always frame as
a question, never a verdict. The receipt rule is non-negotiable here — name the evidence, and invite
correction. Honest, not cruel.
E2The Quiet PivotQUIET_PIVOT STANDARD
A person's actual attention has shifted significantly from what they'd describe as their focus —
a drift they haven't consciously registered.
SIGNALS IT CAN DETECT
Divergence between the entity a person names as their "focus" and the entities actually rising in mention
frequency; a new cluster overtaking an old one with no explicit decision event marking the change.
THE COLD-START READ
"If someone asked what you're focused on, I suspect you'd say [stated]. But the last while of
your mail is mostly about [actual]. Not a contradiction exactly — more that your attention
moved and didn't send a memo. Worth knowing where it actually went."
CONFIRMS IF
the rising cluster keeps diverging from the stated
focus.
DISCONFIRMS IF
stated focus and actual attention realign, or were
aligned all along.

F1Building Mode vs. Recovery ModeMODE_CYCLE STANDARD
A person who cycles between high-output building phases and quieter recovery phases.
Knowing which phase they're in explains a great deal about their behavior.
SIGNALS IT CAN DETECT
Activity and commitment-creation events clustering into bursts separated by quiet periods;
emotional_state valence shifting between energized and drained across those periods.
THE COLD-START READ
"You don't run at one speed — and that's not a flaw, it's a rhythm. There are stretches where
you're building hard, and stretches where you go quiet and refill. Reading you well means
knowing which one you're in right now. Looks like [phase], from here."
CONFIRMS IF
the burst-then-quiet rhythm repeats across
multiple cycles.
DISCONFIRMS IF
output is steady-state, with no real oscillation.
F2The NarrowingNARROWING SENSITIVE
The breadth of a person's life — relationships, interests, activities — visibly contracts toward a
single domain over time. Can be healthy focus. Can be a warning sign.
SIGNALS IT CAN DETECT
Declining diversity of active entity clusters over time; rising concentration of mentions in one cluster;
declining mention of relationship and personal-life entities relative to a single dominant domain.
THE COLD-START READ
"Something I'll raise carefully, because it can mean very different things: your world has gotten
more concentrated lately — more and more about [domain], less of the other threads that used
to be there. Sometimes that's focus, and it's good. Sometimes it's worth checking in on. Only
you know which."
CONFIRMS IF
cluster diversity keeps falling and
personal/relationship mentions keep thinning.
DISCONFIRMS IF
breadth returns, or the concentration was a known,
bounded sprint.
HANDLING — THE MOST CARE IN THE LIBRARY
This shape can correlate with isolation or depression. Never alarm, never diagnose, never assume.
Frame as the person's own to interpret. If signals are strong and the personal/relationship threads have
gone quiet, lean gently toward the value of human connection — and remember EYES is not a substitute
for it. Pain is not a thing to be called out. It is a thing to be careful with.

F3Avoidance-via-ResearchAVOIDANCE STANDARD
When the uncomfortable next step gets replaced by more learning, research, or planning —
productive-feeling activity that quietly substitutes for the hard thing.
SIGNALS IT CAN DETECT
Spikes in research and learning topic mentions co-occurring with the approach of a known hard
commitment — a deadline, a sale, a launch; the hard commitment accruing delayed_on while research
activity rises around it.
THE COLD-START READ
"Here's a sharp one, and I mean it kindly: right around the times [hard thing] gets close, your
mail fills up with reading, researching, planning — everything except the hard thing itself.
Learning more can be exactly how we avoid doing the thing we already know how to do.
Worth catching yourself at it."
CONFIRMS IF
research spikes keep coinciding with avoided hard
commitments.
DISCONFIRMS IF
research reliably precedes action, not avoidance —
i.e. it's preparation, not flight.
